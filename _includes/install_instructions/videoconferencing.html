Google Meet is a video service that runs through your browser, making it easy to connect from any device. It has a relatively simple interface, which we will quickly review when the Workshop begins. The link to join is in the collaborative notes document.

Set up your workspace

Like other Carpentries workshops, you will be learning by "coding along" with the Instructors as we explore the cluster. To do this, you will need to have:

  • A connection to the campus network through the VPN. We will do our best to manage the streaming video footprint, but the success of the Workshop depends on running commands on the CTCMS machines directly.
  • Google Meet in a browser window so you can see what the Instructor is presenting.
  • Collaborative notes in a browser tab or window so you can see reference materials, ask questions, and help one another. Note that CodiMD will ask you to log in, without an option to sign up. Whatever you enter the first time becomes your login for the site.
  • A terminal window so you can see what the Instructor is presenting.
This blog post includes detailed information on how to set up your screen to follow along during the workshop.
